# Facilitator Guide
## Practical Risk Identification Workshop (Expanded 2h45m–3h Version)

This guide is designed to support the **expanded, deeper lesson plan**. It includes extra preparation, facilitation techniques for the new exercises, quality labs, and linkage discussions.

### Recommended Tools & Setup

**Screen Sharing Priorities**
- Primary: Risk Register (Excel/Google Sheets) – always visible during live risk building
- Secondary: Case Study + Inputs Checklist
- Tertiary: Quality checklist or before/after examples (for refinement exercises)
- Keep chat visible and active at all times

**OBS / Streaming Scenes (Recommended)**
1. **Intro / Title scene** (with repo link + objectives)
2. **Main working scene** (spreadsheet + chat overlay – most of the session)
3. **Document scene** (Case Study + Inputs Checklist during review sections)
4. **Examples / Quality Lab scene** (weak vs strong statements, scoring examples)
5. **Wrap-up & Call-to-Action scene**

**Technical Preparation Checklist**
- [ ] Risk Register template open, formatted, and tested with data validation
- [ ] Case Study fully internalized (you should be able to summarize without reading)
- [ ] 6–8 strong example risks prepared (backup only)
- [ ] 3–4 deliberately weak/vague risk statements ready for live refinement exercises
- [ ] Inputs Checklist ready to mark live during the process section
- [ ] Simple quality criteria checklist visible (Specific? Business impact? Owner? Source?)
- [ ] Disclaimer slide ready: “Educational demonstration only – adapt to your organization’s context”
- [ ] Links to templates and repo ready to share in chat

### Chat Engagement Prompts (Expanded)

**During Context & Process sections**
- “What’s the biggest problem you see with risk registers in your organization?”
- “Which input type do you find hardest to turn into useful risks?”

**During Live Risk Building**
- “What else could cause this event?”
- “Is the impact specific enough for a Board or regulator to care?”
- “Who should realistically own this risk (role, not person)?”
- “Does this feel too technical? How do we translate it into business/regulatory impact?”
- “What would an auditor want to see as evidence for this risk?”

**During Quality Review & Refinement**
- “How would you strengthen this statement?”
- “Is the Cause clear and grounded in our available inputs?”
- “Would this risk actually drive action or treatment decisions?”

**During Linkages Discussion**
- “How would this risk change your Vulnerability Management priorities?”
- “Would this risk affect your go-live decision or change advisory board?”
- “What residual risk treatment would you recommend?”

**When a suggestion is vague or weak**
- “Let’s refine that together into Cause → Event → Impact.”
- “Great idea – now let’s make the business impact explicit.”

### Facilitation Techniques for the Expanded Sections

**Running the Mini-Exercise (Input → Risk)**
- Pick one real-looking input (e.g., an audit finding or vulnerability scan summary).
- Ask chat/audience: “What risk could this create?”
- Immediately refine one suggestion live into full Cause → Event → Impact format.
- This builds confidence before the main case study.

**Running the Quality Lab / Refinement Moments**
- Present 1–2 deliberately weak statements.
- Ask the group: “What’s missing or weak here?”
- Guide them through strengthening it (add specific cause, business impact, owner, source).
- Show before/after side-by-side if possible.

**Handling Energy & Timing in a Longer Session**
- The live risk-building block (60–70 min) is the heart – protect this time.
- If energy is high, let the quality review and linkages discussion run longer.
- If energy drops, move faster through context and spend more time on live examples.
- Always leave 15+ minutes for wrap-up, practice challenge, and Q&A.

**Common Pitfalls & Live Corrections**

| Pitfall | How to Correct Live |
|---------|---------------------|
| Vague risk (“Cloud is risky”) | Ask: “What specifically could go wrong and why would leadership care?” |
| Purely technical impact | Push for regulatory, financial, customer, reputational, or operational consequences |
| No owner assigned | Force a realistic role (e.g., “Cloud Migration Lead / CISO”) |
| Missing source | Ask: “Which input or document points to this risk?” |
| Overly broad or duplicate risks | Merge or scope them during the quality review |
| Chat suggests too many risks at once | Prioritize quality – aim for 6–7 strong, well-documented risks |

### After the Stream / Workshop

- Upload the populated (anonymized) Risk Register as a community resource
- Share the full repository link prominently in chat and description
- Collect feedback: Which sections felt most valuable? Which risks resonated most?
- Note recurring questions for future sessions or follow-up content (Risk Analysis, Treatment, etc.)

**Pro tip:** The practice challenge at the end (“Write 4–5 real risks from one of your current initiatives this week”) is one of the highest-value takeaways. Reinforce it strongly.
