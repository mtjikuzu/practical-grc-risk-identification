# Facilitator Guide
## Practical Risk Identification Workshop

### Recommended Tools & Setup

**Screen Sharing**
- Primary: Risk Register Excel / Google Sheets
- Secondary: Case Study document and Inputs Checklist
- Keep chat visible at all times

**OBS / Streaming Scenes (suggested)**
1. Intro / Title scene
2. Main working scene (spreadsheet + chat overlay)
3. Document scene (for Case Study and Checklist)
4. Break / Q&A scene

**Preparation Checklist**
- [ ] Risk Register template open and tested
- [ ] Case Study loaded and summarized in your own words
- [ ] 6 example risks prepared (use as backup if chat is quiet)
- [ ] Inputs Checklist ready to mark live
- [ ] Disclaimer ready: “Educational demonstration only – not professional advice”

### Chat Engagement Prompts

During risk building:
- “What else could cause this event?”
- “Is the impact specific enough for a Board or regulator to care?”
- “Who in a real organization should own this risk?”
- “Does this risk feel too technical? How do we make the business impact clearer?”

When a suggestion is vague:
- “Let’s refine that into Cause → Event → Impact together.”

### Common Pitfalls to Watch For

| Pitfall | How to Correct Live |
|---------|---------------------|
| Vague risk (“Cloud is risky”) | Ask for the specific cause and the business consequence |
| Purely technical impact | Push for regulatory, financial, customer, or reputational impact |
| No owner assigned | Force a role to be named |
| Missing source | Ask “Where did this risk come from?” |
| Too many risks at once | Focus on quality over quantity (5–7 strong risks is better than 20 weak ones) |

### Timing Flexibility

- If chat is highly engaged, extend the live risk-building section.
- If energy drops, move faster through the quality review and spend more time on real-world challenges.
- Always leave at least 10–15 minutes for wrap-up and template sharing.

### After the Stream

- Upload the populated register (anonymized if needed) as a community resource.
- Share the GitHub repository link prominently.
- Collect feedback on which risks resonated most for future sessions.
