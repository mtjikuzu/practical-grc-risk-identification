# Lesson Plan: Practical Risk Identification
## Cause → Event → Impact (Scenario-Based Walkthrough)

**Duration:** 2 hours 45 minutes – 3 hours  
**Format:** Live interactive walkthrough (Twitch / YouTube)  
**Primary Tools:** Risk Register template (Excel/Google Sheets), Case Study document, Inputs Checklist

---

### Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, participants will be able to:

1. Explain how real organizations identify risks during major technology change initiatives.
2. Write clear, specific, and actionable risk statements using the **Cause → Event → Impact** structure.
3. Populate a professional Risk Register using realistic organizational inputs.
4. Assign appropriate Risk Owners and capture sources of information.
5. Recognize common quality problems in risk statements and how to fix them.

---

### Pre-Session Preparation (Facilitator)

- [ ] Open the Apex Financial Services Case Study and be fully familiar with it.
- [ ] Have the blank Risk Register template open and ready to screen-share.
- [ ] Have the Risk Identification Inputs Checklist ready.

### Detailed Timed Agenda

#### 00:00 – 00:15 | Context & Real-World Alignment
**Goal:** Establish credibility and relevance.

- Welcome and session objectives.
- Brief explanation of why organizations perform structured risk identification (regulatory expectation, audit evidence, informed decision-making, prioritization of limited resources).
- How this activity fits into ISO 27001 Clause 6.1 (Information security risk assessment) and the broader risk management process.
- Show a short “lived-in” example of a real risk register (anonymized) so participants see the end product.

**Screen share:** Risk Register template (blank) + short example of a completed register.

#### 00:15 – 00:35 | The Practical Process Organizations Actually Use
**Goal:** Demystify the process.

Walk through the real-world sequence:
1. Define scope and objectives of the risk identification exercise.
2. Gather available inputs (assets, incidents, audits, threat intel, project documents, regulatory requirements).
3. Facilitate identification (workshops, interviews, structured brainstorming).
4. Document risks in a consistent format (Cause → Event → Impact).
5. Assign preliminary Risk Owners.
6. Capture sources and notes for auditability.

Highlight common organizational realities:
- Risk identification is usually triggered by change, not just the annual cycle.
- Perfect data is rarely available — we work with what we have.
- The goal is useful, actionable risks, not academic perfection.

**Screen share:** Risk Identification Inputs Checklist + simple process flowchart.

#### 00:35 – 01:55 | Live Hands-On Demo (Core of the Session)
**Goal:** Demonstrate the full process in real time.

1. **Introduce the Case Study** (5–7 min)  
   Summarize Apex Financial Services, the mobile banking + cloud migration initiative, the aggressive timeline, and the available inputs.

2. **Confirm Scope** (3 min)  
   Explicitly state what is in scope for this exercise.

3. **Review Available Inputs** (8–10 min)  
   Screen-share the Inputs Checklist and mark which sources are available in the Apex scenario. Discuss how each source can generate risk statements.

4. **Live Risk Identification** (55–65 min)  
   Build 5–7 risks live on screen. For each risk:
   - State the Cause
   - State the Event
   - State the Impact (business-focused)
   - Enter the full statement into the Risk Register
   - Assign Category, Affected Assets/Processes, Impact Categories
   - Give initial Likelihood and Impact ratings
   - Assign a realistic Risk Owner (role)
   - Note the source of the risk

   Encourage chat participation:
   - “What other causes could lead to this event?”
   - “Is the impact statement specific enough?”
   - “Who should own this risk?”

5. **Quick Quality Check** (5 min)  
   Review the register for vague language, missing owners, or purely technical impacts without business consequence.

#### 01:55 – 02:20 | Real-World Challenges & Quality Practices
**Goal:** Move from theory to practical maturity.

Cover:
- Vague vs. specific risk statements (show before/after examples).
- The danger of “risk register theater”.
- How mature organizations keep the register alive (triggers, ownership, regular review).
- Linking identified risks to Vulnerability Management findings, change management, and third-party risk processes.
- How auditors typically examine risk identification evidence.

#### 02:20 – 02:45 | Wrap-up, Artifacts & Next Steps
- Summarize key takeaways.
- Share the blank template, the populated sample, the Case Study, and the Inputs Checklist.
- Call to action: “Take one real initiative in your organization this week and try writing 3–5 risks using this method.”
- Tease the next session: Risk Analysis / Evaluation and Risk Treatment.
- Q&A.

---

### Facilitation Tips

- Keep the energy practical and conversational.
- When chat suggests a risk, refine it live into Cause → Event → Impact format.
- If a risk is too technical, push for the business impact.
- If a risk is too vague, ask “What specifically could go wrong and why does the business care?”
- Always assign a role as owner, never leave it blank.

---

### Success Indicators

- Participants can write a clear Cause → Event → Impact statement without prompting.
- The live Risk Register contains specific, business-relevant risks with owners.
- Chat is actively refining language and suggesting additional risks.
- Viewers download the templates and indicate they will try the method at work.
