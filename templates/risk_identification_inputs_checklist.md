# Risk Identification Inputs Checklist

Use this checklist to gather the information that real organizations actually use when identifying risks for a major change initiative.

## 1. Business & Strategic Context
- [ ] Current business objectives and strategic initiatives
- [ ] Approved project scope, timeline, and success criteria
- [ ] Key performance indicators and risk appetite statements (if available)
- [ ] Board or executive priorities related to the initiative

## 2. Asset & Process Information
- [ ] Asset inventory (systems, data stores, applications, people, facilities)
- [ ] Critical business processes and their owners
- [ ] Data classification and data flow diagrams (especially customer financial data)
- [ ] Dependencies (internal systems and third parties)

## 3. Historical & Operational Data
- [ ] Incident and near-miss log (last 12–24 months)
- [ ] Previous risk registers or risk assessment reports
- [ ] Internal and external audit findings
- [ ] Vulnerability scan / penetration test results
- [ ] Change management records and failed changes

## 4. Threat & External Intelligence
- [ ] Relevant threat intelligence reports
- [ ] Industry reports on mobile banking and cloud risks
- [ ] Known attack patterns against financial apps and cloud environments
- [ ] Regulatory guidance and recent enforcement actions

## 5. People & Third Parties
- [ ] Roles and responsibilities (RACI if available)
- [ ] Third-party / vendor risk assessments
- [ ] Skills gaps related to new technology (cloud, mobile security)
- [ ] Training status of staff involved in the change

## 6. Controls & Governance
- [ ] Existing policies, standards, and procedures
- [ ] Current control inventory relevant to the scope
- [ ] Previous management review or risk committee minutes

**How to use in a live session:** Screen-share this checklist and mark which inputs are available in the Apex case study, then demonstrate how each input helps generate specific risk statements.
